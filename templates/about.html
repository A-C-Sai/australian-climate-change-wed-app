About
this
project

Human activities such as fossil fuel use, deforestation, and industrial processes are contributing to rising temperatures, extreme weather, rainfall variability, and habitat disruption.

These shifts directly impact sectors like agriculture, wildlife conservation, insurance, and urban development. However, the lack of accessible and unbiased data can make informed planning difficult.

This project addresses that challenge by:

  • Delivering unbiased and data-driven visualizations of climate change over time.

  • Bridging the gap between scientific data and practical application.

  • Empowering diverse stakeholders to plan proactively and sustainably.

Our mission is to equip key Australian stakeholders including insurance providers, wildlife conservators, farmers, and infrastructure planners with the tools they need to understand and respond to the effects of human-induced climate change.

Using historical climate data (1970–2020) from the Bureau of Meteorology (BOM), this web application turns complex datasets into clear, actionable insights. By providing both high-level summaries and deep-dive analysis tools, we aim to support climate resilient planning, risk assessment, and conservation efforts across Australia.
